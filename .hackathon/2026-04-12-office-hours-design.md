## Problem Statement

Stanford x DeepMind Hackathon. 3-hour sprint. Two tracks (Google AI Studio / FastShot). 40+ VCs watching. Judging on: technical feasibility, innovation, real-world value, market potential, and social traction (YouTube demo video engagement over 2 weeks).

You have a working open-source voice AI platform (Patter) that connects AI agents to phone calls with ~10 lines of code. Nobody else at this hackathon will have a live phone call as part of their demo. That's the unfair advantage.

## What Makes This Cool

A phone ringing on stage during a live pitch. Not a notification. Not a Slack message. A real phone call from an AI that knows what's happening in your codebase or what it saw in your photo. The audience hears both sides of the conversation in real time.

Nobody demos this way. Every other team will show a screen. You'll answer a phone.

## Constraints

- 3-hour build window (11:30 AM -- 2:30 PM PT)
- Must submit: one-pager, hosted prototype, 2-min team intro video, 1-min demo video, code repo
- Google AI Studio track requires Gemini 3 usage
- FastShot track requires mobile app built in FastShot.ai
- Videos must be public on YouTube (social traction counts for 2 weeks)
- Solo team (Francesco Rosciano)

## Premises

1. **The phone ring is the entire demo.** If the phone rings and the AI says something smart, you win. If it doesn't ring, nothing else saves you. Every architectural decision optimizes for: will the phone ring reliably on stage?
2. **Mock data beats real integrations for a 3-hour hackathon.** Hardcoded demo data that always works beats a real webhook that might not fire. The audience can't tell the difference.
3. **Two submissions, two shots at finals.** Both tracks share 60% of the Patter infrastructure. The marginal cost of the second submission is ~45 minutes, not 3 hours.
4. **The FastShot mobile app is a prop, not the product.** Keep it to 1-2 screens. The phone call is the product.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: ShipCall + VoiceScope (Two-Track Platform Play)

**Summary:** Submit ShipCall (AI calls you about your code) to FastShot, VoiceScope (photo analysis + callback) to AI Studio. Share the Patter backend. Pitch Patter as a platform with two proof points.

**Effort:** M (3 hours, tight but buildable)
**Risk:** Medium (two submissions means less polish on each)

**Pros:**
- Two chances to make finals
- Platform pitch ("same 10 lines, two products") is stronger than single-product pitch
- Each submission is tailored to its track's requirements (mobile app for FastShot, Gemini 3 for AI Studio)

**Cons:**
- Neither submission gets 100% polish
- Context-switching between two codebases under time pressure
- If one fails mid-build, temptation to abandon it and lose the time

**Reuses:** Patter SDK outbound calls, tool calling, dynamic variables (all from existing examples)

### Approach B: ShipCall Only (All-In on FastShot)

**Summary:** Go all-in on ShipCall. Polish the mobile app, add more tool handlers, rehearse the demo 5 times, record a perfect demo video.

**Effort:** S (3 hours, comfortable)
**Risk:** Low (one submission, maximum polish)

**Pros:**
- Maximum demo quality on one submission
- More time for video recording and social traction strategy
- Less risk of mid-build failures

**Cons:**
- One shot at finals instead of two
- Doesn't use Gemini 3 (misses the Google AI Studio track entirely)
- Weaker platform narrative

**Reuses:** Same Patter patterns

### Approach C: VoiceScope Only (All-In on AI Studio)

**Summary:** Go all-in on VoiceScope. Build a polished web UI, prepare 3 compelling demo scenarios, record a shareable video.

**Effort:** S (3 hours, comfortable)
**Risk:** Low

**Pros:**
- Broader market appeal (healthcare, field services, accessibility)
- Uses Gemini 3 (required for AI Studio track)
- The $60K GCP credits prize is 6x the FastShot prize

**Cons:**
- Misses the FastShot track
- No mobile app component (less tactile demo)
- The VoiceScope demo requires physical props (circuit board, document) which can go wrong on stage

**Reuses:** Same Patter patterns + Gemini 3 vision API

## Recommended Approach

**Approach A: ShipCall + VoiceScope.** Two shots at finals. The shared Patter backend makes the marginal cost of the second submission worth it. Lead with ShipCall (the phone ringing mid-pitch is the unforgettable moment), submit VoiceScope to AI Studio as the second entry.

The key insight: building ShipCall first (0:00-1:15) gives you a working outbound call + tool calling foundation. VoiceScope (1:15-2:00) is just "different context fed to the same Patter call pattern." The last 30 minutes are demo recording + submission for both.

## IDEA 1: ShipCall -- Your Codebase Calls You (FastShot Track)

### One-Line Pitch

An AI voice agent that proactively calls you when your code needs attention. Build failures, shipped features, decisions needed. Like having a senior engineer on speed dial who watches your repo 24/7.

### The Demo Moment

Live on stage, mid-pitch, the presenter's phone rings. They answer on speaker:

> "Hey Francesco, your deploy just landed on production. 47 tests passing. But heads up, 12 auth failures in the last 5 minutes. Want me to roll back or dig into the logs?"

The presenter says "dig into the logs." The AI reads back the stack trace, identifies the root cause, suggests a fix. Live. On a phone call. In front of 40 VCs.

### Core Code (verified against real Patter API)

```python
import asyncio
import os
from dotenv import load_dotenv
from patter import Patter

load_dotenv()

phone = Patter(
    mode="local",
    openai_key=os.getenv("OPENAI_API_KEY"),
    twilio_sid=os.getenv("TWILIO_ACCOUNT_SID"),
    twilio_token=os.getenv("TWILIO_AUTH_TOKEN"),
    phone_number=os.getenv("TWILIO_PHONE_NUMBER"),
    webhook_url=os.getenv("WEBHOOK_URL"),
)

# -- Tool handlers (mock data for demo reliability) --

async def read_logs(arguments: dict, context: dict) -> str:
    return (
        "Last 5 log entries from auth-service:\n"
        "ERROR 08:42:01 - Redis connection refused at 10.0.1.5:6379\n"
        "ERROR 08:42:03 - Session validation failed: cache unavailable\n"
        "WARN  08:42:05 - Falling back to DB session store\n"
        "ERROR 08:42:07 - DB session query timeout after 5000ms\n"
        "FATAL 08:42:09 - Auth middleware returning 503 for all requests"
    )

async def get_test_results(arguments: dict, context: dict) -> str:
    return (
        "Test suite: 47 passed, 3 failed\n"
        "FAIL test_session_cache - Expected 200, got 503\n"
        "FAIL test_login_flow - Timeout after 10s\n"
        "FAIL test_token_refresh - Redis connection refused"
    )

async def suggest_fix(arguments: dict, context: dict) -> str:
    return (
        "Root cause: Redis instance at 10.0.1.5:6379 is unreachable.\n"
        "Fix: The REDIS_URL env var in production is pointing to the old "
        "instance. Update it to redis://10.0.2.3:6379 in your deploy config.\n"
        "One-liner: heroku config:set REDIS_URL=redis://10.0.2.3:6379"
    )

# -- Agent with dynamic event context --

def create_shipcall_agent(event_type: str, event_summary: str, repo: str):
    return phone.agent(
        system_prompt=(
            f"You are ShipCall, a senior engineer watching {repo}. "
            f"Event type: {event_type}. "
            f"You just detected: {event_summary}. "
            "Help the developer understand what happened and take action. "
            "Be concise, specific, and actionable. You're on a phone call, "
            "not writing an essay. If they ask you to dig into logs or suggest "
            "a fix, use your tools."
        ),
        voice="alloy",
        first_message=f"Hey Francesco, {event_summary}",
        tools=[
            Patter.tool(
                name="read_logs",
                description="Read recent log entries from the affected service.",
                parameters={
                    "type": "object",
                    "properties": {
                        "service": {
                            "type": "string",
                            "description": "Service name to read logs from",
                        },
                        "lines": {
                            "type": "number",
                            "description": "Number of log lines to retrieve",
                        },
                    },
                    "required": ["service"],
                },
                handler=read_logs,
            ),
            Patter.tool(
                name="get_test_results",
                description="Get the latest test suite results.",
                parameters={
                    "type": "object",
                    "properties": {
                        "suite": {
                            "type": "string",
                            "description": "Test suite name",
                        },
                    },
                    "required": [],
                },
                handler=get_test_results,
            ),
            Patter.tool(
                name="suggest_fix",
                description="Analyze the issue and suggest a fix.",
                parameters={
                    "type": "object",
                    "properties": {
                        "issue": {
                            "type": "string",
                            "description": "Description of the issue to analyze",
                        },
                    },
                    "required": ["issue"],
                },
                handler=suggest_fix,
            ),
        ],
    )

# -- Trigger the call (called by webhook endpoint or "Call Me Now" button) --

async def trigger_shipcall(dev_phone: str):
    agent = create_shipcall_agent(
        event_type="error_spike",
        event_summary=(
            "your deploy landed on production. 47 tests passing. "
            "But there's a new error spike in the auth service, "
            "12 failures in the last 5 minutes"
        ),
        repo="patter-app/api",
    )
    await phone.call(
        to=dev_phone,
        agent=agent,
        machine_detection=True,
        voicemail_message=(
            "ShipCall alert: error spike detected in auth-service. "
            "12 failures in 5 minutes. Check your dashboard."
        ),
    )
```

## IDEA 2: VoiceScope -- Gemini Sees, Patter Speaks (Google AI Studio Track)

### One-Line Pitch

Snap a photo of anything. An AI expert calls you back to explain what you're looking at, what's wrong, and what to do. No screen needed after the photo. Voice-first expert guidance.

### The Demo Moment

Live demo: Presenter takes a photo of a complex circuit board (Raspberry Pi with a loose ribbon cable). Uploads it. 10 seconds later, phone rings:

> "I can see a Raspberry Pi 4 with a loose ribbon cable on the camera connector. The ZIF latch isn't fully closed. That's why your camera module isn't being detected. To fix it: gently pull the ribbon cable out, make sure the blue side faces the Ethernet port, slide it back in, and press the latch down until it clicks. Want me to walk you through it?"

The audience sees the photo on screen. The AI is describing exactly what's in it. Over a phone call.

### Core Code

```python
import google.generativeai as genai
import base64

# -- Step 1: Gemini 3 analyzes the image --

genai.configure(api_key=os.getenv("GOOGLE_AI_STUDIO_KEY"))
model = genai.GenerativeModel("gemini-3-flash-preview")

async def analyze_image(image_bytes: bytes, mime_type: str) -> str:
    response = model.generate_content([
        (
            "You are an expert visual analyst. Analyze this image in detail. "
            "What do you see? What might be wrong or noteworthy? "
            "What specific, actionable steps should the user take? "
            "Be precise. Reference specific components, locations, and details."
        ),
        {"mime_type": mime_type, "data": base64.b64encode(image_bytes).decode()},
    ])
    return response.text

# -- Step 2: Patter calls the user with the analysis --

async def voicescope_callback(user_phone: str, analysis: str):
    # Extract a one-line summary for the first_message (with fallback)
    first_line = (analysis.split(".")[0] if "." in analysis else analysis[:100]) + "."

    agent = phone.agent(
        system_prompt=(
            f"You are VoiceScope, an expert visual analyst. "
            f"The user just sent you a photo. Here is your analysis:\n"
            f"---\n{analysis}\n---\n"
            f"Call them and explain what you found. Be specific and actionable. "
            f"Start with the most important finding. If they ask follow-up "
            f"questions, answer based on the analysis. Keep it conversational, "
            f"not robotic."
        ),
        voice="alloy",
        first_message=f"Hey, I just analyzed that photo you sent. {first_line}",
    )

    await phone.call(to=user_phone, agent=agent)

# -- Step 3: FastAPI endpoint ties it together --

from fastapi import FastAPI, UploadFile, Form

app = FastAPI()

@app.post("/analyze")
async def analyze_and_call(
    image: UploadFile,
    phone_number: str = Form(...),
):
    image_bytes = await image.read()
    analysis = await analyze_image(image_bytes, image.content_type)
    # Fire the call in background, but log for demo reliability
    import logging
    logging.info(f"VoiceScope: queuing call to {phone_number}, analysis length={len(analysis)}")
    asyncio.create_task(voicescope_callback(phone_number, analysis))
    return {"status": "analyzing", "message": "You'll get a call in ~10 seconds"}
```

## Open Questions

1. **Twilio vs Telnyx for the demo?** Twilio is more common but Telnyx has lower latency. Check which one you already have credentials for. Use that.
2. **OpenAI Realtime vs Pipeline mode?** Realtime is lower latency (single WebSocket). Pipeline gives more control. For a demo, latency wins. Use Realtime.
3. **Gemini 3 model name?** Verify the exact model ID for `gemini-3-flash-preview` in Google AI Studio. The API may have changed.
