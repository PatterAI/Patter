## Recording Tips

### Environment
- Record in a quiet room with minimal echo
- Use a plain background (clean wall, desk, or blurred background)
- Ensure consistent, soft lighting from the front (no backlight from windows)

### Audio
- Use an external microphone if available (even earbuds are better than laptop mic)
- Do a 10-second test recording and play it back before the full take
- Speak clearly at a moderate pace — slightly slower than conversational

### Video 1 (Team Intro)
- Frame from chest up, camera at eye level
- Look directly into the camera lens, not the screen
- Practice the script 3-5 times to internalize the flow — avoid reading
- Keep energy high but professional — this is for VCs, not a classroom
- Aim for natural confidence, not performance

### Video 2 (Demo)
- Use screen recording software (OBS Studio, macOS built-in, or Loom)
- Set screen resolution to 1920x1080 for clean capture
- Close all notifications and unrelated tabs before recording
- Move the cursor deliberately — avoid erratic mouse movements
- Pre-load the demo state so there are no loading delays during recording
- If using voiceover, record audio separately for cleaner quality and sync in post

### Upload
- Upload to YouTube as **UNLISTED** initially
- Switch to **PUBLIC** at submission time (social traction is a judging criterion)
- Add a clear title: "LEO - Adaptive Learning Platform | Stanford x DeepMind Hackathon 2026"
- Include a brief description with team name and hackathon reference
- Add thumbnail with LEO branding if time permits
